TRANSITIONING MEDICALLY

Medical transition usually involves some combination of Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) and surgery. There are risks associated with medical transitioning, and you should ensure you have researched the options using reputable sources, like the ones linked above, before making any decisions.
Medical transition is available on the NHS, however due to long wait times many individuals choose to undergo self-medication (DIY). In the UK, both masculinizating and feminizing hormones are legal to use and purchase.

FITNESS

If you’re interested in fitness, the Pleasance Sports Complex (which houses the University’s gym) has gender neutral bathrooms and changing facilities. Many trans students are active members of sports clubs; however, the British Universities and Colleges Sport (BUCS) which oversees competitive sport at the national level has a Transgender Policy which may exclude some trans people from competing.
Trans inclusion policies also vary across the various national sporting bodies, so if you have questions, we recommend contacting individual clubs or the Sports Union, who oversee all sports clubs at Edinburgh.

SCHEMES AND SUPPORT SYSTEMS

- LGBTQ+ Peer Mentoring is a scheme, supported by the Students’ Association, which connects LGBTQ+ students with a queer student mentor who can offer informal support and signposting

- The University has a range of health and wellbeing services available, including the Student Counselling Service, which offers students short-term counselling

- The professional caseworkers at the Students’ Association’s Advice Place can offer free, impartial and confidential advice on a wide range of topics, from housing to finances, as well as complaints to the University, and reporting discrimination within the University and beyond
